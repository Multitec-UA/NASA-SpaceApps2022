The sun is a 4.5 billion-year-old star that is 93 million miles (150 million kilometers) from Earth. It has a surface temperature of 27 million degrees Fahrenheit (15 million degrees Celsius). The sun is about 1.3 million times the size of Earth and has the mass of about 333,000 Earths, making it the largest object in the solar system. The sun is the closest star to Earth and is the center of our solar system. The sun provides light and day during the day. It also drives the water cycle and gives us warmth. The sun is a medium-sized star and is about halfway through its life. It will eventually turn into a red giant and then into a white dwarf. The sun is made up of hydrogen and helium. It produces energy through nuclear fusion. The sun releases a tremendous amount of energy, which we receive as sunlight. This energy fuels the Earth's climate and weather. It also supports photosynthesis, which is how plants make their food. The sun is uniquely placed to be the centerpiece of our solar system. It has the right size and mass to support life on Earth. Without the sun, there would be no life on Earth! Thank you for reading! I hope this article helped you learn more about our sun.
