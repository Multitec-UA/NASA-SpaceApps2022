The Sun.

  • October 02, 2022
  • Solar sistemThe sun

The Sun is a star that is located at the center of our solar system. It is a huge ball of hydrogen and helium that is 4.5 billion years old.

The Sun is extremely important to us because it is the source of all life. Without the Sun, there would be no life on Earth. The Sun is also the largest object in our solar system. Its gravity holds everything in our solar system together. The Sun is so big that it would take 1.3 million Earths to fill it up. The hottest part of the Sun is its core, where temperatures can reach up to 15 million degrees Celsius. The Sun is constantly active, and its eruptions and charged particles can influence the nature of space throughout the solar system. NASA and other international space agencies study the Sun 24/7 in order to better understand it. Sun-exploring spacecraft include Parker Solar Probe, Solar Orbiter, SOHO, ACE, IRIS, WIND, Hinode, Solar Dynamics Observatory, and STEREO.
